ABOUT

My name is Tiago. I am in my late 20s and I’m originally from Lisbon, although I’m currently living in Slovenia. I work for Renaissance Periodization (RP) and I have my own coaching business where I help people with their training and fitness goals.

I’ve always been somewhat of a nerd, so when I started my fitness journey almost 10 years ago, that led me to learn everything I could about it. I was obsessed with it, learning about training and nutrition every single day. I initially started with Youtube videos, but soon started to read books and blogs. Eventually, that progressed to textbooks, scientific articles, and several courses and certifications.

I got particularly interested in exercise and nutritional science and making sure my methods were evidence-based rather than just following traditional unproven practices. Especially since like many others, I ended up following poor advice and making me spin my wheels. There is nothing more frustrating than putting in hours of work trying to achieve your goal but not making any progress. The more I learned the better I was able to improve my own results. I want to help you avoid misinformation and get to your goal in the most efficient manner possible.

BENEFITS

Why should you hire a coach? It’s a very reasonable question. You can absolutely get stronger, leaner and fitter on your own. But coaching has some unique advantages that might make the progress a lot easier.

ADVICE

Nowadays you can search for anything on the internet, but without a solid foundation of knowledge, it’s incredibly hard to know what is good information.
What foods should you eat? How many calories do you need? How much training volume should you do? Are you doing too much or too little? How should your training be structured? How do you overcome plateaus?
These questions aren’t easy to answer unless you have experience and knowledge, and searching online will give you countless contradictory answers. A good coach can tell you exactly what you need to do and also the reason behind it. You don’t have to constantly be worried about what is legitimate or not.

ACCOUNTABILITY

Most people fail because they simply stop doing what’s necessary to produce the result they want, even if they know what they should do.
When you’re doing something on your own, it’s hard to keep yourself accountable. If you have someone watching for you, you now feel responsible for your own actions. You know someone cares about your progress and wants you to succeed, so you feel bad if you disappoint them. Accountability makes sure you stick to the plan and don’t sabotage yourself.

OBJECTIVITY

It’s hard to be objective about your training and dieting. This is one of the reasons why it’s common for coaches to also get coached by someone else. Even with all the knowledge and experience in the world, it’s impossible to be objective about your own progress.
People often start over-analyzing everything, worrying about every little detail. If their weight isn’t going as it’s supposed to, they’re very inclined to change calories too soon, or if they miss a rep in their training, they immediately think something is wrong with their programming.
They do this because they lack objectivity, they can’t look at themselves without their own biases. This is where a coach comes in: they can analyse your progress with detachment, avoiding unnecessary changes that can harm your long-term progress.

I follow an approach to my coaching based on evidence-based practice (EBP), which optimizes decision-making for medical practitioners. Here I adapted it to evidence-based coaching (EBC).

EBC relies on 3 foundations:

Research Evidence: Decisions must be based on scientific evidence, and the strength of the evidence should be considered. For example, research has consistently shown that high volume is beneficial for hypertrophy.

Coaching Experience: Practitioner experience is paramount and should be synergistic with research for the best outcomes. For instance, I have noticed that low-intensity deloads are often detrimental to strength in a powerlifting context. Reducing fatigue by relative intensity and volume instead seems to work better in my experience.

Client’s Preferences: The client's preferences and values must be taken into account, and the ultimate goal is to help the client reach their own personal goals. While this also applies to training, it’s best seen in nutrition. No matter how good of a plan it is, people aren’t going to follow a diet they don’t like.
